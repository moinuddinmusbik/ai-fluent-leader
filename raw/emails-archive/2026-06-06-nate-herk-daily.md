# Nate Herk Daily Implementation Playbook — 2026-06-06
## AGI is Here. Anthropic Just Proved It.

**Type:** COMMENTARY | **Duration:** 12:37 | **Views:** 48,799

## What Happened

- **The report:** Anthropic published ["When AI Builds Itself"](https://www.anthropic.com/institute/recursive-self-improvement) — an Anthropic Institute paper revealing internal development data on recursive self-improvement.
- **The headline stat:** As of May 2026, more than 80% of the code Anthropic merges is authored by Claude — up from low single digits before Claude Code launched in early 2025.
- **The productivity signal:** Anthropic engineers now ship 8× as much code per quarter as 2021–2024, with two inflection points: Claude running code autonomously (2025), and longer time-horizon models (2026).
- **The capability frontier:** Claude Mythos Preview (internal only): autonomous tasks lasting "at least 16 hours" — at METR's measurement ceiling. Not yet publicly released.
- **The call to slow down:** Anthropic says alignment is the one thing they're "least certain about" and a slowdown would be good — contingent on all major labs pausing and verifying simultaneously.

## Nate's Take

Nate's core claim: AGI by the practical definition is already here. His test: open-ended problem solving where humans supply the goal but not the method. That bar is met at Anthropic today. Not claiming superintelligence — claiming Anthropic's own scenario two (AI does work, humans direct) is the current reality, not a future state.

## His Reasoning (with receipts)

**The three scenarios (from the report):**
1. Trend stalls — AI plateaus as a powerful bounded tool
2. Gains compound — AI does execution, humans set directions and judge results ← Nate says this is NOW
3. AI builds its own successor — progress limited only by compute

**Evidence base:**
- Anthropic internal data: >80% code by Claude, 8× engineer output uplift
- Claude Mythos Preview: 16-hour autonomous task benchmark (METR upper limit)
- Alignment gap: Anthropic admits alignment is what they're "least certain about"

## What It Means For Builders

- Human bottleneck = direction quality, not execution speed. People who set goals clearly are 8× more valuable than two years ago.
- Most org AI strategies built around AI-as-assistant; Anthropic's data describes AI-as-co-engineer.
- Alignment is the real wildcard: capability accelerating faster than alignment research.
- When Mythos ships publicly, every human-hours-per-project estimate goes obsolete.

## What To Watch Next

- Claude Mythos Preview public launch → watch METR time-horizon curve
- OpenAI / Google response to Anthropic's slow-down call
- Anthropic alignment research velocity relative to capability publications
- Scenario-three signals: AI-authored training runs

## Steal This For Your Org

1. **AI-authorship audit** — what % of output is AI-authored? Benchmark this week. <20% = 2025 Anthropic baseline.
2. **"Goal only" experiment** — give a senior person an agent and an open-ended goal (no implementation instructions), measure vs. normal week
3. **Leadership scenario workshop** — read the Anthropic report together, assign a scenario to your 18-month plan explicitly
4. **Pre-plan the Mythos pilot** — define team, task, success metric before it ships

## Mistake to Avoid

Reading the 80% stat as a productivity number. The structural shift is that the human role has migrated from executor to director. Optimizing for execution speed when the scarce resource is now direction quality.
