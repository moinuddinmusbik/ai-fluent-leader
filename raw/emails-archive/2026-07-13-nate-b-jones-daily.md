## The Big Idea

Benchmarks tell you which AI model is smarter. They do not tell you which one fits *you*. Nate argues the moment we have entered — with GPT-5.6 Sol, Fable 5, Grok 4.5, and GLM 5.2 all competitive — is the moment that distinction finally matters. The leaderboard sorts models. Only your own work pattern can tell you which model to open in the morning. His answer: most serious leaders should be running both Sol and Fable, for different jobs, not making a single bet on a winner.

## Why It Matters Now

The model race just entered a new phase: the gap between leading models is narrowing while the gap between *how different people work* remains wide. In a world where Sol, Fable, Grok, and GLM all have credible claims, defaulting to whatever topped last week's leaderboard is increasingly the wrong heuristic. The subscription decision is becoming a strategic one — and leaders who treat it as a single-winner choice will systematically under-leverage the model that actually fits their work pattern.

## His Argument

- **The job is only half of it:** Last week Nate said "start with the job." He now revises that: the job leaves out the person doing the work. The same task will be better served by Sol for one person and Fable for another, based entirely on how they think and prompt.
- **Two distinct model families, not two rankings:** OpenAI's RL-based training bet and Anthropic's pre-training bet produced genuinely different model temperaments. Sol takes the whole instruction seriously, keeps going, and works well inside pre-built skills and tools. Fable excels when the important part of the job is still "sitting somewhere between your intent and your words."
- **Nate's own split:** He benchmarks AI models professionally. Fable finished ahead of Sol on his Dingo suite average. He still opens Sol every morning — because he prompts in long, technical runs, knows his edges upfront, and steers by correcting the work. When the task is still ambiguous, he routes to Fable.
- **Models are becoming families:** As models improve, the reasons different people pick different ones will drift further apart, not converge. The sooner leaders understand their own work pattern, the less the leaderboard matters.
- **[[model-fit]] as the operationalizing tool:** Nate built "Model Fit" — a free tool that asks you how you work and returns a model mix plus prompts calibrated to give your work a boost. The diagnostic reveals your pattern; a two-week test confirms whether you've picked correctly.

## Receipts

- Sol scores **93 on Dingo** (Nate's knowledge-work benchmark) — a new high on Agent's Last Exam; still "the dumber model on my benchmarks" relative to Fable
- **Fable beats Sol on Nate's Dingo suite average** — better at reading between the lines and incomplete intent
- Active model landscape cited: **GPT-5.6 Sol, Fable 5, Grok 4.5, GLM 5.2** — all credible options
- Nate runs **Fable as architect inside Ringer** (his own production system) — the high-ambiguity, high-judgment role
- **Two-week calibration window** built into Model Fit as a concrete falsifiability signal

## The Contrarian Edge

- **Consensus:** Pick the benchmark winner — run the leaderboard, find the top model, switch subscriptions.
- **Nate's break:** Sol is definitionally lower on his benchmarks. He uses it every morning. "Smarter" is not the right axis — fit is. Two people doing the same job but prompting differently should pick different models. The person is a variable the leaderboard ignores entirely.

## What It Means For You

1. **Run the [[model-fit]] diagnostic this week** — use modelfit.natebjones.com, describe how you actually work, get a model mix, and commit to a two-week calibration test.
2. **Stop treating model selection as a team-wide decree** — different people have different work patterns; a portfolio approach (Sol-family + Fable-family, routed by task and person) likely outperforms a single mandate.
3. **Audit how you actually prompt** — are you a "correct-the-output" person or a "let-it-find-the-intent" person? That distinction predicts your model fit.
4. **Design your harness for model portability** — the advantage compounds in the harness, not the model; build it portable across model families now.

## Mistake to Avoid

Letting this week's benchmark release trigger a subscription switch without first asking whether the winner fits how *you* work. A 93 on Dingo is a real score — and Nate still doesn't open that model first. The leaderboard tells you which model is smarter in the abstract; it tells you nothing about whether you'll produce better work with it.
