## Core Message

Nate sits down with Devin Kearns (co-founder & CEO of Custom AI Studio) to unpack what separates AI agencies with real enterprise value from lifestyle businesses that won't survive 2027. The mid-market ($50M–$500M revenue) is the prime opportunity, and the only durable offer replaces one repeatable expensive process end-to-end — priced on outcomes, not hours.

## What He Covers

- **Why most AI services won't survive 2027:** Generic GPT wrappers and one-off automation builds have no moat. Enterprise clients will consolidate to vendors who own a vertical workflow end-to-end.
- **The mid-market sweet spot:** Above $50M revenue, below $500M — real pain, real budget, not yet locked into hyperscaler AI contracts.
- **The $100M offer formula:** Pick one repeatable expensive process. Replace it with a supervised AI loop. Charge on outcome (saved FTE hours, error-rate reduction), never time-and-materials.
- **Claude Code as delivery layer:** Compresses build time from months to weeks; small teams punch above their weight on enterprise deals.
- **Hiring for an AI agency:** No ML PhD needed — process mapping, agent capability matching, and supervised handoff management are the core skills.
- **Pricing & packaging:** Discovery fee ($15–25k) funds the prototype; then retainer tied to outcome metrics.

## As an AI-Fluent Leader — What To Do This Week

1. **Map one repeatable process in your org that costs >$500k/yr.** This is your first AI-native workflow candidate. Scope it as if you were pitching an AI agency to replace it.
2. **Price the displacement, not the tool.** Calculate what it costs today (FTEs × salary + error rework). 30–50% reduction is your value proposition.
3. **Build a 2-hour prototype with Claude Code.** Take the mapped process and build a minimal supervised loop. You don't need perfect — you need demonstrable.
4. **Document the handoff points.** Every AI workflow has moments where a human must review. Identifying those explicitly turns a demo into an enterprise-grade proposal.

## Mistake to Avoid

**Selling capability, not outcomes.** Telling a CXO "we use Claude Code and n8n" is a fast path to a polite no. Frame every offer as: process X costs $Y today; our supervised AI loop reduces it to $Z. Budget owners respond to ROI, not stack details.
