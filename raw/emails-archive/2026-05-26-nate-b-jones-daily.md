## Core Message

Shopify’s River agent only runs in public Slack channels because real organizational learning requires senior people to run actual work where everyone can watch. Private AI usage is quietly creating an apprenticeship gap: junior staff see outputs but never see the reasoning behind them. Teams getting smarter with AI are not just using it faster — they are making their AI process visible enough that it transmits tacit knowledge across the organization.

## What He Covers

- **Shopify’s River agent and the public-channel rule:** Deliberate design that makes AI-assisted work visible to the whole org.
- **The apprenticeship gap:** Senior people using AI privately means junior staff never see judgment calls, pushback, or prompting strategy.
- **Polanyi’s paradox and tacit knowledge:** We know more than we can tell — visible process is the only way to transfer expertise at scale.
- **Four parts of AI work to make visible:** Problem framing, prompt strategy, output evaluation, and the decision to use (or not use) AI output.
- **How regulated teams can adapt:** Declared spaces with privacy labels preserve safety while enabling process visibility.

## As an AI-Fluent Leader — What To Do This Week

1. **Audit your team’s AI usage: how much is private vs visible?** Ask people to share one interaction per week — prompt and reasoning, not just output.
2. **Designate a public AI channel this week.** Senior people must lead by example for the norm of visibility to take hold.
3. **Share process, not just prompts.** A prompt library shares the “what”; visible process transmits the “why”.
4. **Set a “public AI work” goal for Q3.** Define and measure what visible AI process means for your team.

## Mistake to Avoid

Sharing AI outputs instead of AI process. Output-sharing feels like organizational learning but only shares the “what.” The thing that transfers expertise is the reasoning process: how the problem was framed, what alternatives were considered, why one output was chosen.
