## Core Message

AI collapsed the cost of building software — and with it, the PM’s old job of deciding what to build. Microsoft already has over a million power platform assets built internally. The artifact arrives before the request. The new PM job is not prototyping faster; it is classifying software abundance into market value: building a production class ladder and enforcing demotion as rigorously as promotion. Market judgment is now the scarce skill.

## What He Covers

- **The prototype commons:** Anyone can build a working prototype in an afternoon — creating hidden demand and hidden risk simultaneously.
- **Why the non-technical PM is finished:** Prototyping is table stakes; the PM who can’t evaluate whether a prototype should be relied on has lost the core function.
- **The production class ladder:** Prototype → Tested → Production, with explicit demotion criteria.
- **The new PM decision rule:** “Somebody already built something — should the company rely on it?”
- **Why demotion matters as much as promotion:** The risk in a prototype commons is reliance on software that was never production-grade.

## As an AI-Fluent Leader — What To Do This Week

1. **Build a production class ladder for your team’s AI outputs.** Three rungs: Prototype, Tested, Production. Classify what you already have.
2. **Identify which AI-generated assets are being relied on without production status.** This is the hidden risk in your prototype commons.
3. **Practice the new PM decision rule.** For the next 3 requests: “Does something that does this already exist?”
4. **Add demotion criteria to your governance process.** What triggers a production asset moving back to Tested or Prototype?

## Mistake to Avoid

Investing in AI prototyping speed as your career differentiator. When everyone can prototype, the durable edge is judgment and governance: evaluating whether software should be relied on and enforcing the production class ladder.
