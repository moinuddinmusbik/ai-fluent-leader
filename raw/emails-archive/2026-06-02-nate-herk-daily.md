## Core Message

Understanding the full 100-year arc of AI — from Alan Turing cracking Enigma, through two near-death AI winters, to deep learning's comeback and AlphaGo's world-shaking victory — is the credibility baseline every AI-fluent leader needs. History doesn't just explain how we got here; it reveals the repeating patterns (hype → winter → breakthrough) that are unfolding again right now at the organizational level.

## What He Covers

- **The Origin (1920s–1950s):** A 26-year-old in his parents' bedroom — Alan Turing — and the Enigma-cracking Bombe machine that proved computation could simulate reasoning, planting the seed for the entire field.
- **The Two AI Winters:** The field nearly collapsed twice (mid-1970s and late 1980s) as overhyped promises crashed into hardware limits. Nate walks through why each winter happened and what pulled the field back each time.
- **The Written-Off Approach:** Neural networks were dismissed for decades as impractical and unscalable — the approach "everyone wrote off." He traces how researchers quietly kept the faith until compute finally caught up.
- **The ImageNet Moment (2012):** Geoffrey Hinton's deep CNN winning ImageNet by a massive margin was the signal that neural nets had crossed a threshold — triggering the race that produced GPT-4 and Claude.
- **AlphaGo vs. Lee Sedol (2016):** The single move that made a world champion walk away — DeepMind's AlphaGo playing Move 37, a move no human would have played, forcing the world to accept that AI had surpassed human intuition in bounded domains.
- **Where We Are Now:** The current moment is the acceleration end-state of that 100-year arc — and according to Nate, we're "just getting started." Knowing the history is what separates leaders who make clear-eyed AI bets from those chasing hype.

## As an AI-Fluent Leader — What To Do This Week

1. **Build your 60-second AI history pitch.** Practice explaining the two AI winters and why the current wave is structurally different (data scale + transformer architecture + cloud compute). Use it next time someone in your org asks "is AI just another hype cycle?"
2. **Map your org's hype-cycle position.** Every AI winter was a gap between marketing and actual capability. Audit your current AI initiatives — are you in the trough of disillusionment or genuinely post-winter on a capability that now works reliably?
3. **Study the Sedol retirement decision.** Lee Sedol retired from professional Go in 2019, citing AI as "an entity that cannot be defeated." Ask yourself: what is the Go-equivalent domain in your industry, and is AI already there?
4. **Share this video as a team baseline.** Send it to your leadership team as a shared history primer. A 30-minute conversation around this timeline will raise the quality of every AI strategy discussion that follows.

## Mistake to Avoid

Treating AI history as trivia for enthusiasts. The boom–winter–breakthrough cycle is actively repeating at the organizational level right now — teams over-promise, get burned, and cut AI budgets. Leaders who know the pattern can hold their nerve during the trough, spot genuine breakthroughs from noise, and make contrarian bets on approaches that look like dead ends today but have the structural tailwinds to win tomorrow.
