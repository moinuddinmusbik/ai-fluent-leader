## Core Message

AI can build a polished deck in minutes — but polished is not the same as trustworthy. A clean-looking deck with one undefendable number is worse than no deck at all. The unlock is a four-stage workflow (sources → structure → creation → verification) and a “hostile reviewer” prompt that attacks the output before you defend it. Without a truth layer around the file, AI-generated documents create confident-looking errors that slip through review at exactly the worst moment.

## What He Covers

- **A prompt asks for output; a workflow defines trust:** Single-prompt generation looks like a win until the board deck blends actuals and plan data.
- **Four-stage document workflow:** Sources → Structure → Creation → Verification, each with its own trust checkpoint.
- **The hostile reviewer prompt:** A second prompt playing a skeptical senior reviewer surfaces logical gaps and blended data that proofreading misses.
- **The task risk gradient:** AI is lowest risk on formatting, medium on synthesis, highest on numerical reasoning and sourcing.
- **The RALPH loop (Codex + Opus):** Multi-model verification where one model generates and a second audits.

## As an AI-Fluent Leader — What To Do This Week

1. **Adopt the four-stage workflow for your next major deliverable.** Don’t skip sources or structure — that’s where the truth layer is built.
2. **Run the hostile reviewer prompt on a real document before your next presentation.**
3. **Map your team’s work to the task risk gradient.** Add mandatory verification for the top 3 highest-risk AI tasks.
4. **Build a team norm: no AI-generated numbers without sourcing.**

## Mistake to Avoid

Treating AI output as a first draft that just needs light editing. Errors in AI-generated documents are often structural: blended data sources, goal-directed guessing, confident formatting around unverifiable numbers. Editing is not the same as verification.
