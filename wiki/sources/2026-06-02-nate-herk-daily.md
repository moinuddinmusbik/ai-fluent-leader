## Summary

Nate Herk walks through the full 100-year arc of artificial intelligence history — from Alan Turing cracking the Enigma code in World War II, through the two AI winters that nearly killed the field, to the deep learning comeback, AlphaGo's world-changing Move 37, and the current acceleration phase.

The framing is not academic: Nate positions this history as a practical leadership tool. Leaders who understand the hype–winter–breakthrough cycle can make clear-eyed AI bets, hold their nerve during organizational troughs, and distinguish genuine breakthroughs from noise. The video ends with the note that "we're just getting started."

## Key Takeaways

- Alan Turing — working in his parents' bedroom at 26 — cracked Enigma and laid the mathematical foundations for machine reasoning
- The field suffered two near-death AI winters (mid-1970s, late 1980s) when hype outran hardware capabilities
- Neural networks were dismissed for decades as a dead end; the researchers who kept faith were proven right when compute caught up
- Geoffrey Hinton's deep CNN winning ImageNet 2012 by a massive margin was the concrete signal that the threshold had been crossed
- AlphaGo's Move 37 (2016) — a move no human would play — forced the world to accept AI had surpassed human intuition in bounded domains; Lee Sedol retired from professional Go in 2019
- The current moment is the acceleration end-state of that 100-year arc; history provides pattern recognition for navigating it
