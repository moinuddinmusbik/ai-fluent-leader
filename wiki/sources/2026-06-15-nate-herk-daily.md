## What It Is

A ranked breakdown of six AI skills that futureproof any career — regardless of job title. Nate's frame: AI will reshape or replace millions of jobs, but you don't have to switch careers to stay ahead. The six skills span from becoming the go-to AI person on your team through to income diversification as unemployment insurance — the skill most people never think about.

## The Six Skills (From Description, Tags & Chapters)

1. **Skill 1 — Become the AI person (0:29):** Establish yourself as the AI-literate person on your current team. The first commenter to resonate hardest with this noted feeling overwhelmed even running an AI business — Nate validates this; you don't need to know everything, just be willing to figure it out and share what works.
2. **Skill 2 — Context engineering (4:47):** The layer above prompt engineering — structuring rich context (role, task, desired output, constraints) before the AI sees the task. Routes the right context to the right model for useful, not generic, output.
3. **Skill 3 — Build your own Jarvis / AI agents vs. workflows (7:42):** Understanding the agent-vs-workflow distinction (probabilistic vs. deterministic). The Excel analogy: AI agent literacy in 2026 = Excel literacy in 2005. Building one automation that runs without you is the minimum bar.
4. **Skill 4 — Iteration speed (10:13):** Cycling through AI outputs rapidly — prompt, evaluate, refine — rather than accepting the first result. This determines whether AI gives you 1.2× or 10× leverage.
5. **Skill 5 — Taste and judgment / knowing when NOT to use AI (13:13):** The counterintuitive skill. High-trust, relationship-defining, and nuanced judgment tasks stay human. AI is leverage on expertise, not a replacement for it. "The real advantage to AI is combining domain expertise, judgment, and context" (commenter @nicvandewetering, 3 likes).
6. **Skill 6 — Income diversification / job stacking (16:40):** The skill most people never think about. Use AI to build one or more income streams outside your primary job — building in public, AI-assisted content, or a micro-product — as unemployment insurance. Start before you need it.

## Key Takeaways

- Skills 1–5 make you harder to replace in your current role; Skill 6 makes you indifferent to replacement.
- AI is leverage on domain expertise and judgment — not a substitute for either.
- The people who become AI-native within their existing field are far harder to displace than those who pivot to become generic "AI people."
- The compound effect of Skill 6 (building in public) requires runway. Start while employed.
- One commenter flagged a potential Skill 7: "multiplication" — deploying AI tools for colleagues and clients as a versionable, feedback-driven service.
