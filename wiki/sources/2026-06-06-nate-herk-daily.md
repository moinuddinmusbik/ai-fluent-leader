# AGI is Here. Anthropic Just Proved It. — [[nate-herk]] · 2026-06-06

**Type:** COMMENTARY | **Duration:** 12:37 | **Views:** 48,799

[[nate-herk]] reads Anthropic's ["When AI Builds Itself"](https://www.anthropic.com/institute/recursive-self-improvement) report and argues that AGI — by the practical definition that matters — has already arrived, as evidenced by Anthropic's own internal data.

---

## What Happened

- Anthropic published "When AI Builds Itself" (Anthropic Institute) revealing internal development data
- As of May 2026: >80% of Anthropic's merged code authored by Claude (up from low single digits pre-Claude Code launch)
- Engineers ship 8× more code/quarter vs. 2021–2024; two inflection points: Claude running code (2025), longer time-horizon models (2026)
- Claude Mythos Preview (internal only): autonomous tasks lasting "at least 16 hours" — at METR's measurement ceiling
- Anthropic calls for a slowdown contingent on all major labs pausing simultaneously and verifying each other's pause

## Nate's Take

Core claim: AGI by the practical definition is already here. His test: open-ended problem solving where humans supply the goal but not the method. That bar is met at Anthropic today. Not claiming superintelligence — claiming Anthropic's own scenario two (AI does work, humans direct) is the current reality, not a future state. Most AGI debates fight over benchmarks; Nate argues the crossing happened quietly in 2025–2026.

## His Reasoning (with receipts)

**The three scenarios (from the report):**
1. Trend stalls — AI plateaus as a powerful bounded tool
2. Gains compound — AI does execution, humans set directions and judge results ← Nate says this is NOW
3. AI builds its own successor — progress limited only by compute (scenario three)

**Evidence base:**
- Anthropic internal data: >80% code by Claude, 8× engineer output uplift
- Claude Mythos Preview: 16-hour autonomous task benchmark (METR upper limit)
- Alignment gap: Anthropic admits alignment is what they're "least certain about"
- The Mythos number is internal-only; public release will reset expectations again, fast

## What It Means For Builders

- Human bottleneck = direction quality, not execution speed. People who set goals clearly and judge results critically are 8× more valuable than two years ago
- Most org AI strategies are built around AI-as-assistant; Anthropic's data describes AI-as-co-engineer — team ratios and role definitions haven't caught up
- Alignment is the real wildcard: capability accelerating faster than alignment research — Anthropic's own admission
- When Mythos ships publicly, every human-hours-per-project estimate goes obsolete. Teams with scaffolding absorb it; others scramble

## What To Watch Next

- Claude Mythos Preview public launch → watch METR time-horizon curve when it ships
- OpenAI / Google response to Anthropic's slow-down call — silence or engagement, both informative
- Anthropic alignment research velocity relative to capability publications
- Scenario-three signals: AI-authored training runs appearing in papers or disclosures

## Steal This For Your Org

1. **AI-authorship audit** — what % of output is AI-authored? Benchmark this week. <20% = 2025 Anthropic baseline.
2. **"Goal only" experiment** — give a senior person an agent and an open-ended goal (no implementation instructions), measure vs. normal week
3. **Leadership scenario workshop** — read the Anthropic report together, assign a scenario to your 18-month plan explicitly
4. **Pre-plan the Mythos pilot** — define team, task, success metric before it ships so you absorb it in days not months

## Mistake to Avoid

Reading the 80% stat as a productivity number. It's a structural signal: the human role has migrated from executor to director. Optimizing for execution speed when the scarce resource is now direction quality.
