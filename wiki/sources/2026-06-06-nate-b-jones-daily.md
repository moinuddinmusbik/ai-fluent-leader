# 2026-06-06 — Nate B Jones Daily — The Most Expensive AI Mistake Isn't Prompting

**Source:** [[nate-b-jones]] · YouTube + Substack · 2026-06-06

## Thesis

The most expensive AI mistake organizations make is not a prompting failure — it is the failure to build an organizational rejection-encoding layer. Frontier models beat domain professionals on raw output 70% of the time, yet outputs consistently fail domain review. The gap is not model capability; it is organizational taste infrastructure.

## Key Takeaways

- **Rejections are data, not dead ends.** Every time a team discards an AI output without capturing why, it loses institutional knowledge that would improve every future run.
- **Three dimensions of taste:** Recognition (spotting what's wrong), articulation (naming it precisely), encoding (writing it into a reusable prompt constraint or system instruction).
- **The Epic Systems model:** Epic scaled clinical taste through thousands of encoded workflows. The AI-era equivalent is building a systematic rejection-to-constraint pipeline.
- **Structural tooling gap:** No major AI product has a native rejection-capture layer. This is an open organizational design problem every leader must solve manually today.
- **Output ≠ acceptance.** Benchmark superiority does not equal domain acceptance. The two metrics diverge sharply in expert domains, and that divergence is where organizational taste lives.
