## The Big Idea

Claude Fable 5 is the first model that is bigger than our habits. For three years the model was the ceiling you learned to ask underneath. With Fable, Nate hit a different wall: he kept running out of work he knew how to hand over before the model ran out of ability. The constraint has migrated from model intelligence to human task imagination — and the leaders who see that shift first win back serious time.

---

## Why It Matters Now

Fable 5 went live, proved the capability claim, and was pulled offline by US government directive within days. It will return — and the people who spent the interim building the skill of asking bigger will extract far more value. More importantly, this capability level is the leading edge of what lands in the next ChatGPT and open-weight models arriving months behind it. The window to develop the skill is now.

---

## His Argument

- **The model stopped being the limit:** Nate handed Fable a deliberately poisoned database — ghost records, corrupt files, planted traps — went to do something else, and came back to find the work done: a clean database with garbage quarantined, plus an unprompted review queue for every call the model was unsure about.
- **The new bottleneck is ask-size:** What failed wasn't the model. It was Nate running out of "big things to ask for." Three years of prompting trained a habit of asking small. Fable makes that habit actively wasteful.
- **"Detailed task imagination" is the new core skill:** The ability to see whole jobs — not prompts — that a model can carry start to finish. Concrete, learnable, and almost nobody is teaching it.
- **The Whole-Job Spec as delivery mechanism:** A nine-field spec Nate fills out before handing over anything real: job definition, success criteria, failure modes, review trigger, output format. Forces you to see delegation, not instruction.
- **Fatigue is evidence, not cynicism:** AI feeling smaller than the headlines is not hype — it is the size of the ask.

---

## Receipts

- **Poisoned database test:** Ghost records, corrupt files, planted traps, cleaned end-to-end with a self-built review queue, unsupervised.
- **Stripe:** Compressed months of engineering work into days using large-model capabilities.
- **"Overkill" reviews are technically correct:** They accurately describe the size of the ask, not the model's ability.
- **Prompt-stacking recipes fail:** Nate tested methods to reconstruct Fable-level capability via system prompt engineering or stacked smaller models. None held.

---

## The Contrarian Edge

- The standard narrative is benchmarks and intelligence. Nate rejects that framing explicitly: "The new model changes everything" is the most exhausted sentence on the internet.
- His actual claim is a skills story: what you bring to the model matters more than what the model brings to you.
- The government pull is irrelevant to the lesson: building the task-imagination skill during the offline period is the strategically correct move.

---

## Framework: [[whole-job-spec]]

Nate's nine-field spec for delegating a whole job to a frontier model (from Substack guide, paywalled):
- Fields include: job definition, start state, success criteria, failure modes, review trigger, output format, and more.
- Converts a prompt into a delegation; forces the user to think in outcomes not instructions.
- Key shift: prompt mode (one-step) vs. work mode (whole-job spec handed over).

Also references [[detailed-task-imagination]] — first introduced 2026-06-23.

---

## What It Means For You

1. **Run a job audit on your calendar.** Identify three recurring tasks where you currently prompt AI one step at a time. Rewrite one as a whole job with a start state, finish state, and failure mode — then hand it over completely.
2. **Build one Whole-Job Spec before your next AI session.** Even filling out five of Nate's nine fields will fundamentally change what you ask for.
3. **Frame the coming months as practice time, not waiting time.** Fable 5 is offline. Open-weight and next-gen models arriving in Q3 will match this capability level. Leaders who develop the task-imagination habit now win.
4. **Audit your team's AI usage through the prompting-vs-delegating lens.** If your people are sending one-step prompts, the bottleneck is ask-size — a training and culture problem you can fix before the hardware improves.

---

## Mistake to Avoid

Using a frontier model exactly as you use a cheaper model — quick summaries, one-step rewrites, a code snippet. "You will feel a slow, expensive model doing fine work that a cheaper model does faster. The early reviews calling it overkill aren't wrong. They're accurately describing the size of the ask." — Nate B. Jones (Substack preview)
