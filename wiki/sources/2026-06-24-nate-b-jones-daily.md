## The Big Idea

The real reason AI hasn't reduced cognitive load for most leaders isn't model quality — it's architecture. The app era atomized recurring work across a dozen surfaces and turned humans into the integration layer between them. The fix isn't a smarter chatbot. It's a **loop of loops**: a set of narrow recurring jobs that carry memory, operate within defined boundaries, and notice when a change in one domain should trigger work in another.

## The Framework: Prompt → Loop → Loop of Loops

- **Prompt:** A one-time ask. You drive context every time. Useful for discrete, well-defined tasks.
- **Loop:** A recurring job with memory, diet (data sources), boundaries (stop conditions), and a review cadence. The obligation carries itself forward. The loop knows what changed since last time.
- **Loop of Loops:** A control layer where loops notice each other. Cross-domain dependencies — rain changes packing, late pickup changes the calendar — live in the system rather than in your head. This is where the integration burden the app era created is finally lifted.

## How It Works

- **Four components:** Job · Diet · Boundaries · Review
- **Working example:** The school trip — email + weather + pickup time + calendar conflict + outgrown raincoat across 5 different apps. A loop of loops connects them and surfaces the cross-domain update.
- **Safe build pattern:** Start with draft-and-stop for consequential decisions. "Does it before asking" only for genuinely reversible low-stakes tasks.
- **Five-questions test:** Nate provides a diagnostic questionnaire (in the full Substack) that structures loop design: what it monitors, what changed, what it can't touch, where it stops, who reviews.

## Where It Applies

- Sales / customer follow-up loops (what happened, what changed, who owns next step, what can't be promised)
- Research and monitoring loops (source tracking, threshold alerts)
- The Friday build (one recurring weekly job, diet + boundary, draft output for review)
- Household baseline loops as low-stakes training ground

## What It Means For You

1. Audit cross-domain obligations (email + calendar + Slack + judgment = loop target), not individual tasks
2. Before any agent: define Diet → Boundary → Review cadence — no loop without stop conditions
3. Draft-and-stop mode for 2 weeks before granting autonomous action on consequential decisions
4. Let single loops run first; cross-loop dependency architecture reveals itself through use

## Mistake to Avoid

Deploying a loop before defining its boundaries. The stop condition is the primary safety mechanism — not a constraint on usefulness.
