## The Big Idea

On July 1, Fable 5 came back online after 18 days offline — but the real signal was what the outage revealed. The leaders who were never disrupted owned the harness and routed by job type. In a world where any model can be throttled, repriced, capped, or pulled behind a policy filter overnight, the skill is not picking a favorite model: it is routing the right intelligence to the right job, so that when one pipe closes, you already know where the work goes next.

## The Framework: Job-First Routing

Named [[job-first-routing]] — a 3-tier + specialist system. Define the job before picking the model.

- **Tier 1 — Daily Driver:** The model you reach for before the task is fully defined. Must handle messy, mixed, judgment-heavy work. Breadth is the requirement; harness quality matters as much as model quality.
- **Tier 2 — Cheap Workhorse (GLM 5.2):** Center-of-distribution work — familiar shapes, repeatable, easy to review: meeting summaries, standard PowerPoints, landing page drafts, CRM cleanups, routine synthesis, familiar code. GLM 5.2 often beats Claude on these at a fraction of the cost.
- **Tier 3 — Frontier Model (Claude, ChatGPT):** [[fable-style-problem|Fable-style problems]] — when the shape of work is not obvious yet, generalized intelligence is required, or judgment and taste are load-bearing. "There is no substitute for a frontier model" on novel, non-standard tasks.
- **Tier 4 — Specialists:** Images (Flux, Zimage, Grok Image), Video (LTX → Seed Dance API → Grok for clips), Live Web (Grok for X posts). Learn the job first, then walk to the model name.

## How It Works

- Ask job questions first (coding? decks? synthesis? discovery?) — the answers route to a tier before any model name enters the conversation.
- Familiar vs. novel: known shape + easy review → Cheap Workhorse; unclear shape, high stakes, first-time domain → Frontier.
- **Harness quality is not separable from model quality.** Gemini's intelligence is strong but its harness is difficult; Nate has de-emphasized it in his own stack. GLM 5.2's launch of Z.AI as a native harness is the signal — Chinese open-source models are pursuing harness quality.
- Test before committing: run your actual inputs through any candidate model before declaring it a daily driver.

## Receipts

- **Coinbase:** Increasing token usage while decreasing costs via smart routing to open-source (GLM, Kimmy).
- **Cursor:** Built on Kem; migrated to a pre-trained model for savings without slowing down.
- **Lindy:** Moving serious traffic to DeepSeek.
- **Shopify & Airbnb:** Qwen-style routing, queries land there effectively.
- **Microsoft:** Testing into DeepSeek architecture.
- **Fable 5 return terms:** Usage caps, credit model, filter to weaker model — the model you relied on is not guaranteed to be the same model tomorrow.
- **Gemini verdict:** "Strong intelligence, weak harness" — de-emphasized in his own usage.

## The Contrarian Edge

- The GLM 5.2 conversation is dominated by coding examples "because coding is easy to benchmark." But the real savings are in center-of-distribution *business* work: PowerPoints, memos, CRM cleanups, routine synthesis — the majority of what office workers actually produce.
- "Don't copy what someone else is doing. And that includes me." He explicitly declines to give universal model prescriptions.
- Five rules of the road: (1) don't copy others; (2) ask how *hard* the work is, not how much you need; (3) know how to evaluate output; (4) make model choice itself not be work; (5) don't pick too many models.

## What It Means For You

1. **Map your five recurring artifacts.** Assign each to a tier. This is your personal routing card.
2. **Stop paying frontier prices for center-of-distribution work.** Familiar shapes belong on GLM 5.2 or equivalent.
3. **Own the harness, not the model.** Build around a harness you control (Claude Code, Codex, open-source). Re-routing should be a five-minute task, not a crisis.
4. **Test with real inputs before declaring a daily driver.** Don't validate on hypotheticals — run the actual work.

## Mistake to Avoid

Picking a model before defining the job. Nate: "I am seeing a trap that a lot of people are falling into where they confuse the act of picking a model with getting real work done." Fable's 18-day outage exposed exactly who had made this mistake.
