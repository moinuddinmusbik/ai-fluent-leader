## What Happened

- **Guest:** Ailin — 15-year email developer, laid off at 39 with two young kids, no technical AI background.
- **Outcome:** ~1 year later, Head of AI at a company with 15 brands, owning AI strategy across all of them.
- **Method:** Learned n8n and Claude Code from scratch. Built in public. Walked into interviews with a portfolio of real projects.
- **Pivot question:** Interviewer asked: *"What have you built?"* — she had a concrete, demonstrable answer.

---

## Nate's Take

The new credential in the AI era is not a degree, a certification, or a job title — it is a body of real, documented work. The "What have you built?" test is becoming the universal bar for AI roles, and the only way to pass it is to have built something real. The path Ailin took — n8n + Claude Code, building in public, treating every project as a proof-of-concept — is reproducible by any professional willing to start before they feel ready.

## What It Means For Builders

- **The role you're waiting to be given is already available.** Companies with multi-brand/BU structures are hiring first-movers with demonstrated builds, not tenured specialists.
- **n8n + Claude Code is the minimum viable stack.** No prior coding experience required. Produces employer-credible outputs fast.
- **The 15-brand scope is the opportunity signal.** One AI strategy role spanning 15 business units = massive leverage ratio for an AI-fluent hire.
- **Layoff risk and AI opportunity are the same event.** The force that displaced Ailin was the one she used to re-enter at a higher level. This is the pattern.

## Steal This For Your Org

1. **Run the "What have you built?" screen** on every AI-role candidate. Ask for a link to see the work.
2. **Give non-technical team members a real automation challenge** with n8n + Claude Code this week.
3. **Document and share AI builds** publicly or internally — accumulate visible evidence of capability.
4. **Map the "15-brand" opportunity** in your own structure. Identify whether a single AI strategy layer could own multiple BUs.

---

## Mistake to Avoid

Waiting until you feel technically "ready" before building or sharing. Ailin's advantage was a documented trail of builds started *before* she was an expert. The people collecting certifications while waiting for the right moment will hand the "Head of AI" role to the person who posted their messy first n8n workflow six months ago.
