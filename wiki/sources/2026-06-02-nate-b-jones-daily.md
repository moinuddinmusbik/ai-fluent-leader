# Why Your Meetings Are Destroying Your Output — The Five-Person Strike Team Framework

**Routine:** Nate B Jones Daily Leader Briefing
**Date:** 2026-06-02
**YouTube:** [Why your meetings are actually destroying your output](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hlGWME2hsl4)
**Substack:** [Executive Briefing: When Each Person Produces $2M a Year, the Sixth Team Member Costs Millions in Lost Productivity](https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/executive-briefing-ai-raised-output)

## Summary

Nate B. Jones makes a structural argument rooted in combinatorics: AI raised per-person output by an order of magnitude while leaving coordination costs completely untouched. The n(n−1)/2 formula — the number of communication channels in a team — means that each additional team member adds a proportionally higher coordination burden. When individual output reaches $2M/year equivalent, the coordination tax on a sixth team member costs millions in lost productivity. The structural response is not to cut headcount but to cap team size at 4–5 and dramatically expand mission ambition.

Jones introduces two structural modes for the AI era. **Scouts** handle uncertain, exploratory terrain where the mission is unclear and wide latitude is needed. **Strike teams** handle defined missions requiring tight, coordinated execution. Most organizations are running strike team work with scout-team staffing (or vice versa), which is a leading cause of the meeting overload and output-correctness gap leaders currently experience.

A third concept — the **Steinberger Threshold** — describes the dividing line between people who direct AI agents and those who get directed by them. This threshold, not title or tenure, is the new talent segmentation line for AI-era organizations.

## Key Takeaways

- AI raised output per person by ~10x but reduced coordination cost by $0. The n(n−1)/2 math now punishes team size with new severity.
- The real bottleneck shifted from volume to **correctness**. Optimizing for output volume while neglecting correctness produces teams that ship broken things fast.
- Every initiative should be classified as **Scout** (uncertain, exploratory) or **Strike Team** (defined, execution-ready) before staffing begins.
- The **Steinberger Threshold** is the new talent segmentation line: can this person direct AI agents toward correct output, or do they defer to the agents?
- The AI era's real opportunity is expanding ambition with the same 4–5 people — not extracting cost savings through headcount reduction.

## Notable Data Points

- Combinatorics: a 5-person team has 10 communication channels; a 6-person team has 15. AI amplified output but not coordination efficiency.
- Per-person output at $2M/year scale makes the sixth team member structurally unaffordable in coordination cost.
