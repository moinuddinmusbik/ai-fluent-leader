# Cheap software made your PM job harder, not easier. Here’s the new job.

**Routine:** [[nate-b-jones]] Daily Leader Briefing | **Date:** 2026-05-29
**YouTube:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b6J387xJvHg
**Substack:** https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/product-management-cheap-software-governance

## Summary

AI collapsed the cost of building software and eliminated the scarcity that made the traditional PM role legible. Microsoft already has over a million power platform assets built internally. The artifact arrives before the request. In this environment, the question “Should we build this?” is replaced by “Somebody already built something — should the company rely on it?”

The new PM role is classifying software abundance into market value. This requires a production class ladder (Prototype → Tested → Production) with explicit demotion criteria, and the judgment to apply it under conditions of constant inbound. Nate argues that demotion — moving assets back down the ladder when they stop meeting production standards — is as important as promotion, and that non-technical PMs who only prototype have lost the job’s core function.

## Key Takeaways

- AI collapsed the cost of building software; the bottleneck shifted from building to evaluating
- Microsoft has 1M+ internal power platform assets — the prototype commons already exists
- The new PM decision rule: “Should the company rely on this?” not “Should we build this?”
- Production class ladder: Prototype → Tested → Production, with explicit criteria at each rung
- Demotion matters as much as promotion — reliance on unqualified assets is the hidden risk
- Market judgment is now the scarce PM skill; prototyping speed is table stakes
