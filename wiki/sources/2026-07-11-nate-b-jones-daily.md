## The Big Idea

Two people use the exact same AI on the exact same Tuesday. One spends the morning cleaning up a slide deck that came back 80% right. The other writes an 11-minute specification, treats the model as an autonomous agent, goes for coffee, and returns to six polished deliverables before lunch. The gap isn't the AI — it's the skill of how you hand work to it: the [[capability-dissipation-gap]].

---

## His Argument

Nate's core thesis: **the AI skill nobody talks about is not prompting; it is specification writing combined with treating AI as an autonomous agent rather than a chat interface.** The people who close this gap at the individual level are already capturing disproportionate returns. At the economy-wide level, this same dynamic — the Capability-Dissipation Gap — explains why market panic over AI is systematically mislabeled: it's not doom, it's dissipation lag.

---

## The Framework — [[capability-dissipation-gap]]

The **Capability-Dissipation Gap** operates at two scales:

**Individual level:**
- Chat-window mode (Person 1): quick request → 80% result → manual cleanup. Marginal net gain.
- Autonomous-agent mode (Person 2): 11-minute real specification → hands off → returns to finished work → repeats 6× before lunch.
- The skill: specification writing + treating AI as an autonomous agent, not a respondent.

**Economy-wide level:**
- The formal economic gap between what AI can technically do and what the economy has actually reorganized around AI doing.
- Widened by four inertia forces: Regulatory, Organizational, Cultural, Trust.
- This gap explains why both the AI doom timeline (Citrini scenario) and the AI boom timeline are wrong — the dissipation absorbs the disruption unevenly.

## The Contrarian Edge

- **Against the doom consensus:** The formal economic conditions for AI-caused contraction are too extreme — what's happening (IBM/COBOL moment) is dissipation lag, not a genuine employment collapse signal.
- **Against the prompting consensus:** The primary AI skill is not prompting; it is escaping the chat-window frame entirely through specification writing.
- **Market incoherence as opportunity:** Simultaneously pricing AI as too weak and too strong creates a structural window for leaders who understand the capability-dissipation curve.

---

## What It Means For Leaders

1. Audit specification practice — invest 10–15 min writing a real spec before each AI task
2. Measure the team's capability-dissipation ratio — low ratio = process bottleneck, not tool bottleneck
3. Reframe AI market risk for boards — dissipation lag, not doom, explains large market moves
4. Invest in gap-closing roles: specification writers, autonomous-handoff designers, judgment holders

## Mistake to Avoid

Treating prompting as the primary AI skill. Prompt-engineering training without specification discipline keeps teams in 80%-result/20%-cleanup loops — net productivity gain erased.
