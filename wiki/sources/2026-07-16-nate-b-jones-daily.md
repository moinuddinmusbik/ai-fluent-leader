## The Big Idea

You have quietly become the integration layer for your AI stack. Every tool is great at one slice of the work, and you are the one shuttling context from one to the next — re-explaining your role, your project, your intent for the fifth time. The models got powerful. What they got wrong is memory and intent. The fix is not another tool. It is building a personal memory-and-intent layer so agents act from *your* context instead of guessing — and stop where *you* tell them to.

---

## The Framework — [[one-loop]] (The Five-Part Loop)

The five parts of the minimum viable agent loop, per Nate:

| Part | Layer | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Memory** | [[owned-stack]] / Open Brain | What the agent knows about you — owned by you, not the platform |
| **Method** | [[owned-stack]] / Open Skills | How you want work done — your patterns, not the app's defaults |
| **Boundary** | [[one-loop]] | Where the agent stops and asks — the most underbuilt part |
| **Receipt** | [[one-loop]] | Proof of what it did and why — accountability at action level |
| **Judgment** | [[one-loop]] | Whether to proceed or pause — drafting vs. sending |

> "The smallest unit that lets an agent act for you without guessing." — Nate B. Jones (from Substack post)

## How It Works

**The [[owned-stack]] architecture (three layers):**
1. **Open Brain** — your memory, stored in a database you control
2. **Open Skills** — your methods, written as instructions
3. **Open Engine** — the agent that moves the work

**Principle:** *Rented intelligence on top, owned context underneath.*

**Build path (as of mid-2026):** Point Claude Code or Codex at the Open Stack guide. The coding agent reads the guide, prepares the SQL, debugs the MCP connections, and walks the setup — while you keep control of accounts, permissions, secrets, and final approval. The build is now ~5× easier than it was earlier in 2026 because agents can build agents.

## The Contrarian Edge

- **Don't wait for platforms:** Waiting for OpenClaw, Hermes, Apple Intelligence, OpenAI, or Anthropic means surrendering control of what the AI remembers about you, on their schedule, on their terms.
- **Capability → Intent shift:** The industry focuses on what agents *can* do. Nate reframes: the hard problem now is whether the agent understood what you *wanted*. A capable agent that misreads intent doesn't hand back a wrong answer — it sends the email.
- **One narrow loop beats a whole assistant:** A single tight loop with owned context, clear method, explicit boundary, and a receipt outperforms a general assistant that owns nothing.

---

## Mistake to Avoid

Adding more AI tools before building the memory and boundary layer. Each new tool without shared context increases your integration burden. The "one more tool" pattern compounds with every addition.

---

## What It Means For You

1. **Name your integration tax** — count daily re-explanations and copy-paste handoffs; that number is the business case for a memory layer.
2. **Build Boundary before capability** — Boundary and Receipt are engineering priorities #1 and #2, not afterthoughts.
3. **Use a coding agent to build the stack** — Claude Code or Codex as your contractor; you as the architect who approves every final action.
4. **Start with one loop, not a whole assistant** — pick the one recurring situation you're most tired of explaining and ship the five-part loop around it this week.
