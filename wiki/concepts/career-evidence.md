# Career Evidence

What counts as **proof of professional value** — the artifacts, behaviors, and demonstrations that signal competence and judgment to employers and peers.

## The Shift

Pre-AI, career evidence was primarily **output-based**: polished decks, reports, code, and deliverables demonstrated mastery because they were hard to produce without genuine skill.

In AI-abundant environments (2026+), output-based evidence has lost its signal. Microsoft’s data shows 86% of professionals already treat AI output as a starting point — meaning anyone with AI access can produce identical polished artifacts. The evidence layer has collapsed.

The replacement is **reasoning-based evidence**: live demonstrations of judgment under pressure, documented decision-making processes, and visible thinking that can’t be delegated to AI.

## High-Signal Evidence (AI Era)

- **Whiteboard sessions**: Live, unscripted problem-solving that reveals real-time reasoning
- **Judgment documentation**: Explicit records of why decisions were made, what alternatives were considered, and what tradeoffs were accepted
- **Four portable judgment questions**: Nate B. Jones framework for capturing transferable reasoning

## Low-Signal Evidence (AI Era)

- Polished documents, decks, reports (AI can produce identical output)
- Code that “just works” without explanation
- Portfolios of finished artifacts without reasoning traces
