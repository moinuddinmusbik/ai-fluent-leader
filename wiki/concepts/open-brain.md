# Open Brain

[[nate-b-jones]]'s earlier framework (Substack: "Every AI You Use Forgets You — Here's...") arguing that personal and professional memory — notes, transcripts, Slack threads, calendars, meeting summaries, project histories — had already moved into software, but stayed trapped inside whichever AI product captured it first. His claim: your memory should be yours, portable across tools, not reset every time you switch apps or models. "Whoever owns the memory owns the starting point."
