# Scout vs. Strike Team

A two-mode framework introduced by Nate B. Jones for classifying every AI-era initiative into the correct structural form before staffing begins. Mis-classifying an initiative — running a scout project with a strike team, or vice versa — is a primary cause of meeting overload, coordination failure, and output-correctness gaps.

## The Two Modes

**Scouts** — for uncertain, exploratory terrain:
- Mission is not yet well-defined
- Wide latitude and judgment required
- Small, high-autonomy individuals or pairs
- Output is discovery, not delivery
- Success metric: learning, not shipping

**Strike Teams** — for defined, execution-ready missions:
- Mission parameters are clear
- Tight coordination, focused scope
- 4–5 people maximum (see [[five-person-strike-team]])
- Output is delivery, not discovery
- Success metric: correct, on-target delivery

## Application

Every active project should be classified before staffing: "Is this mission defined well enough for tight execution, or does it require exploratory judgment?" The answer determines the structure, the team size, and the success metrics.
