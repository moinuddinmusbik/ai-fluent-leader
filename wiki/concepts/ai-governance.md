# AI Governance

The structures, processes, and accountability mechanisms that organisations put in place to ensure AI systems are deployed responsibly, transparently, and within risk tolerances.

## The 2026 Governance Gap (IBM IBV)

As of July 2026, enterprise AI governance is structurally failing:
- **2/3 of CIOs/CTOs** are accountable for AI systems they don't fully control
- **70%** say business teams deploy AI faster than IT can track (shadow AI)
- **77%** report AI adoption is outpacing governance capacity
- **Only 11%** feel fully ready for agentic AI scale
- **Only 8%** of U.S. public companies disclose board-level AI oversight

## Governance Design Patterns

### 1. Control by Design (IBM recommendation)
Embed control, monitoring, and override mechanisms at the point of system design — not as post-deployment audits.

### 2. Shared Platform Governance (HDFC Bank / Cognizant)
Centralise AI infrastructure so governance rules apply uniformly across all deployments rather than being negotiated department-by-department.

### 3. Structurally Independent Oversight Body (Anthropic LTBT)
An oversight body whose members have no financial stake in commercial outcomes — trustees hold no equity, share no profits, are chosen independently of management.

## Board-Level Governance

- Only 8% of U.S. public companies disclose board-level AI oversight as of 2026
- Leading practice: AI governance committees with multi-disciplinary membership (technologists, economists, legal, risk, operations)
- Reference model: Anthropic's Long-Term Benefit Trust ([[anthropic]])
