# AI Fluency

The capacity for a leader to make confident, well-informed decisions about AI — its capabilities, limits, organizational implications, and where it fits into strategy. Distinct from being a hands-on builder.

## Working definition (to refine)

An AI-fluent leader can:
- Read a model capability claim and judge its near-term business impact
- Tell apart strategic AI moves from implementation noise
- Communicate AI direction to engineers, exec teams, and boards
- Decide where AI changes the business model vs. where it just speeds existing work

## Why it matters

The user is pursuing AI leadership roles (CAIO, Head of AI). AI fluency is the durable skill underneath those titles — the wiki is built around developing and demonstrating it.

## Open questions

- Is AI fluency one skill or three (technical literacy + strategic judgment + communication)?
- What does someone need to *show* (artifacts, decisions, builds) to credibly claim AI fluency?
