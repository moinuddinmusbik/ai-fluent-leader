# Detailed Task Imagination

A framework coined by [[nate-b-jones]] to name the core skill that big-model AI (Fable-class and beyond) requires from humans.

## Definition (his words)

> "The ability to look at your own work and see the whole job that an AI could do if it had the right context and the right tools and a clear picture of what done looks like."

## Why it matters

Prompt engineering emerged as a skill because models were small — you had to fit your ask inside their limit. Detailed task imagination is the inverse: the model is now bigger than your habits, so the constraint shifts to whether you can see work large enough to give it.

## How it differs from delegation

Delegation concerns tasks already in your tracker — named, assigned, bounded. Detailed task imagination is about *spotting* the unlisted, gnarly, nobody-owns-it work that was never assigned because it felt too big or too ambiguous. That is the class of work that Fable-sized models are built to carry.

## Qualifying signal

If a piece of work makes you sigh and face-palm, it qualifies. Examples Nate gives:
- Merging 2 million customer CRM records (dedup, stale accounts, account briefs, reproducible)
- Fact-checking and aligning a 500-page board packet
- Processing 40,000 reviews
- Refactoring an entire code repository

## The skill in practice

1. Write down what is stressing you about work — the "weather around your work"
2. Pick the most valuable unlisted job
3. Build a data pack: assemble all source material, write a clear paragraph defining "done"
4. Hand over the job; walk away (resisting the 3-year habit of hovering)
5. Review the output like an owner reviewing a senior stakeholder's work
