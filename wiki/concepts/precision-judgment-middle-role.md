# Precision-Judgment Middle Role

Nate B. Jones's framing for the functional role that survives when AI agents can code continuously for months and review contracts autonomously.

## Definition

The middle role sits between the vague business need and the machine executing it. It requires two capabilities:

1. **Spec precision** — translating a vague, ambiguous business requirement into a specification precise enough to direct an agent to produce useful output.
2. **Customer judgment** — evaluating whether what the agent produces actually solves the real problem for a real customer; not whether it compiles or runs, but whether it serves the underlying need.

"Precision on one side, judgment on the other. Title doesn't matter." — Nate B. Jones (from Short teaser, 2026-07-09)

## Why it survives agent coding

When code is nearly free to produce, the writing and reviewing of code are displaced — but the upstream translation work (vague → precise) and downstream evaluation work (output → real value) cannot yet be done without human understanding of business context and customer reality. The middle role is where those two irreducible human functions live.

## Application

- Applies to any discipline where someone converts a stakeholder request into deliverable output, then validates it — not just software.
- The function, not the title, determines whether a role is in the displacement zone.
