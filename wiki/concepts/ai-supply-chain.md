# AI Supply Chain

The **physical industrial infrastructure** underneath every AI model and API: high bandwidth memory (HBM), advanced packaging, power infrastructure, cooling, and the construction timelines that govern all of them.

## Why It Matters

AI is commonly treated as a software business with a scalable backend. The reality is that the backend runs on physical components with their own supply chains, capital cycles, and capacity constraints — none of which respond to software-speed iteration.

Microsoft’s $190B CapEx commitment (2026) and the phrase “capacity constrained” in vendor communications reveal that AI is industrial infrastructure. Every hyperscaler is committing $80–90B annually to the same constrained supply base, creating systemic allocation risk.

## Key Physical Constraints

- **High Bandwidth Memory (HBM):** 90% of chip die area is packaging vs 12% logic — packaging bottlenecks cannot be resolved at software speed
- **Advanced packaging and substrates:** TSMC-dependent, running on 18-month production cycles
- **Power and cooling:** Data center construction timelines of 2–3 years
- **Capital cycle:** 18–24 month CapEx cycles, not SaaS quarterly refreshes

## Executive Implications

- AI vendor contracts are supply contracts in everything but name
- “Capacity constrained” language in vendor agreements is a strategic risk signal
- Token forecasting replaces seat licensing as the correct AI budgeting unit
- Developers belong in procurement — model choices determine supply chain dependencies
- Supply assurance exercises belong in AI investment reviews alongside ROI analysis
