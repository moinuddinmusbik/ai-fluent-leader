# Bottleneck Migration

The observation — and diagnostic framework — that AI has *relocated* (not eliminated) the primary bottleneck in organizational work.

## Core Premise

Every organization operates on an implicit answer to: **"What's expensive here?"** That answer has historically been *execution* (hours, skills, people). Every major process ritual — PRDs, alignment meetings, approval gates, Agile — was built to protect and rationalize expensive execution.

AI made execution nearly free. The bottleneck migrated upstream into:

| New Scarce Resource | Why It Didn't Get Cheaper |
|---|---|
| **Clarity** | Knowing what's worth building requires judgment, not compute |
| **Ambition** | Vision for what could exist is a human faculty |
| **Distribution** | Earning attention and trust at scale can't be automated away |
| **Relationships** | Compounding trust is irreducibly human |

## The Inversion Signal

The cheapest test of whether your team has adapted: **which takes longer — writing the spec, or building the prototype?** If the spec still takes longer, you're still operating on the old cost structure. The rational sequence has flipped: act → learn → plan, not plan → approve → act.

## Habits to Retire

- **Permission loops** — waiting for sign-off before prototyping
- **Polish-as-hiding** — over-refining to delay shipping
- **Meetings-as-accountability-theater** — synchronizing on things AI can simply do
