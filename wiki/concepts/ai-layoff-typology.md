# AI Layoff Typology

A five-category framework introduced by [[nate-b-jones]] for classifying AI-era layoffs as strategy signals, rather than treating them uniformly as "AI replaced workers."

## The Five Categories

| Category | Example | What it signals |
|----------|---------|-----------------|
| **Hyperscaler** | Meta | CapEx reallocation to compute; headcount cut to fund GPU billions, not AI displacement |
| **Visionary** | Block / Jack Dorsey | Genuine restructuring around an AI-native operating model; the only category to run toward |
| **Activity** | Cloudflare | Usage/engagement data collapsed; cuts mask hidden distress metrics |
| **Hope** | Various | AI narrative + future references + zero operational numbers; most dangerous for job-seekers |
| **Fifth Category** | Various | AI framing is cosmetic; real cause is financial, legal, or organizational |

## Core Principle

A layoff is the loudest strategy signal a company can send. Classify it correctly and it is free intelligence. Misclassify it and you bet your career or capital on the wrong story.

## Diagnostic Questions

1. Is headcount being cut to fund CapEx? → Hyperscaler
2. Is there a published AI operating model with restructured roles? → Visionary
3. Did internal usage data or engagement metrics decline first? → Activity
4. Is the company citing AI without operational numbers? → Hope
5. Does the AI framing hold up under the financial and operational evidence? If not → Fifth Category
