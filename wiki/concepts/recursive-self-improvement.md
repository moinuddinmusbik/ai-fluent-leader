# Recursive Self-Improvement

The capability of an AI system to fully autonomously design and develop its own successor. Defined and used by Anthropic in their June 2026 "When AI Builds Itself" report.

## Anthropic's Three-Scenario Framework

1. **Stalls** — the exponential trend plateaus; AI remains a powerful bounded tool
2. **Compounds (scenario two)** — gains keep accelerating; AI does the execution work, but humans continue to set research directions and judge results ← Nate Herk argues this is already the present (2026-06-06 commentary)
3. **Closes the loop (scenario three)** — AI fully builds its own successor; progress limited only by compute, not human involvement

## Current State (per Anthropic, May 2026)

- >80% of Anthropic's merged code authored by Claude (up from low single digits pre-2025)
- Engineers ship 8× more code/quarter than 2021–2024
- Claude Mythos Preview handles 16-hour autonomous tasks (METR measurement ceiling)
- Alignment described as the "least certain" element — the risk that makes scenario three dangerous
