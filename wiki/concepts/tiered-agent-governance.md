# Tiered Agent Governance

## Definition

Tiered Agent Governance is an enterprise AI governance framework that classifies AI agents by decision impact and data access scope, then applies proportional controls — rather than treating all agents identically (binary: lock-down or full-trust).

Introduced as a critical framework in [[weekly-ai-leadership-stories]] (July 2026 digest), drawing on Gartner's warning that "applying uniform governance across AI agents will lead to enterprise AI agent failure" (May 2026) and Technology Radar July 2026 analysis.

## The Binary Governance Trap

Most enterprises in 2026 are in one of two failure modes:
1. **Over-restriction:** Locking down all agents identically, regardless of their actual risk level — creating bureaucracy that destroys value.
2. **Under-restriction (full-trust):** Treating all agents as benign without oversight — creating liability exposure that Gartner predicts will cause 40% of enterprises to decommission agents by 2027.

## Framework Dimensions

Agents should be classified on two axes:
- **Decision impact:** Low (informational/advisory) → Medium (process automation) → High (financial, legal, customer-facing commitments)
- **Data access scope:** Internal only → Cross-system → External / regulated data

Governance controls — guardrails, audit trail depth, human-in-the-loop thresholds, simulation requirements — should scale with tier level.
