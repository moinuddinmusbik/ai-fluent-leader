# EU AI Act

## Definition
The world's first comprehensive AI regulation framework, structured around a risk-tiered compliance model. Applies to any organization deploying AI affecting EU-based users or employees regardless of country of incorporation.

## Enforcement Timeline
- **Feb 2, 2025:** Prohibited AI practices ban
- **Aug 2, 2025:** GPAI model obligations begin
- **Jun 29, 2026:** EU Council gave final approval to simplification package
- **Aug 2, 2026:** Full enforcement — high-risk AI obligations, GPAI transparency, enforcement powers activated

## Risk Tiers
| Tier | Obligations |
|------|-------------|
| Unacceptable (social scoring, biometric surveillance) | Prohibited |
| High-risk (hiring, credit, infrastructure) | Full documentation, human oversight, risk assessment |
| Limited-risk (chatbots) | Transparency disclosures |
| Minimal-risk | Voluntary code of conduct |

## Penalties
- Up to **€35M or 7% of global annual turnover** — steeper than GDPR's 4%

## Board-Level Implications
- AI inventories, risk classifications, and governance docs must be audit-ready by August 2, 2026
- Assign accountability to CAIO or GC explicitly — ambiguity about ownership is penalized
- Treat as a global governance forcing function: implement standards company-wide
