# Narrative → Correction → Sorting

**Introduced by:** [[nate-b-jones]] · 2026-06-15 episode  
**Alias:** Three Phases of AI Market Cycle

## Definition

A three-phase model for understanding where a technology market is in its maturation cycle:

1. **Narrative phase** — everyone looks like a winner; story and speculation dominate; capital flows broadly
2. **Correction phase** — financial froth gets priced out; stocks fall; skeptics declare the technology was a fraud
3. **Sorting phase** — real AI revenue separates from companies with only AI narratives; durable winners emerge; the infrastructure built during the narrative phase begins generating returns

## Nate's Claim (Jun 2026)

As of June 2026, AI markets are exiting the correction phase and entering the sorting phase. The sorting phase is where competitive advantage is determined — it rewards companies that were building real payback models during the narrative phase, and punishes those that paused during the correction.

## Historical precedent

Nate draws the parallel to railroads, fiber, and dot-com: each had the same three phases. The infrastructure built in the narrative phase endured; the investors who bet purely on narrative did not recover. The companies that used the infrastructure intelligently during and after the sorting phase captured the lasting value.

## Leader implication

The sorting signal to track: a specific process measurably faster, or a specific decision made with better information. If your team cannot name a real-revenue AI story by Q4 2026, you are entering the sorting phase as a laggard.
