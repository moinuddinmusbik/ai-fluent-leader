# Vibe vs. Scar

A legibility framework introduced by [[nate-b-jones]] in the Substack post *"If the AI is a vibe, the human is a scar"* (2026-07-07).

## Definition

- **Vibe (AI):** Smooth, stateless, coherent output generated by pattern-matching without accumulated experiential weight. Consistent, polished, on-brand — and increasingly indistinguishable from produced human content.
- **Scar (Human):** Output carrying the marks of real experience: specific failures recovered from, contextual knowledge only someone who was there could have, visible inconsistency (the stumble, the off day, the batch-filmed shirt) that proves something actually happened to a real person over time.

## The Core Inversion

The synthetic-media era has inverted the trust signal. Previously, polish and consistency signaled competence. Now, in an audience primed to detect AI content, polish and consistency signal *absence of humanity*. The scar — the accumulated, visible mark of real experience — has become the proof-of-work for human presence.

## Practical Test

When evaluating content, a communication, a candidate, or a vendor: does this output show accumulated, specific, contextual knowledge and the marks of real experience (scar)? Or does it feel smooth, stateless, without friction or specificity (vibe)?
