# Monetizing AI Automation

The phase after learning AI automation tools where practitioners convert technical skills into client revenue — niche selection, outreach, offer packaging, discovery calls, and retainer conversion. Nate Herk's explicit content category for the post-tutorial arc, directly linked in the description of his June 24 announcement video.

## Key Aspects

- **Phase definition:** The "Monetizing AI Automation" arc begins after a practitioner can build working AI systems and shift to: what do clients actually pay for, how do I reach them, and how do I turn a project into recurring revenue.
- **The gap Nate is addressing:** Comment signals on the AIS Live announcement show the tension clearly — a viewer who mastered n8n and vibe coding for a year but "struggling to put skills into practice" vs a $25K MRR agency looking for peers. The tools-to-revenue gap is the primary unsolved problem in the no-code AI automation community.
- **AIS Live Day 2 framework (Earn day):** Niche selection → cold outreach that books calls → offer/positioning → discovery call that closes → pricing → delivery-to-retainer conversion. The full arc from unknown to recurring client.
- **Community receipts (2026 data from AIS members):** $60K contract, $24K engagement, $211K deal, $3.3K + $5.2K RAG contracts — the benchmark for what practitioners are closing.
- **Tool War as prerequisite data:** Before pricing offers, practitioners need to know which tools actually make money — the AIS Live "Tool War" slot (n8n vs Claude Code vs Make vs GHL with revenue data) provides the stack-selection foundation.
