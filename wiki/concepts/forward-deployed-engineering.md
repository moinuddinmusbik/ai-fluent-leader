# Forward-Deployed Engineering

## Definition
An organizational model in which AI engineers are embedded directly inside specific business units — rather than a central AI team — to own end-to-end outcomes in that context.

## Why It Works
- Central AI teams produce tooling business units don't adopt because engineers don't understand the workflows
- Forward-deployed engineers are measured on business outcomes (revenue, cost, accuracy), not features shipped
- Embedded ownership accelerates the feedback loop between AI capability and workflow redesign

## Origin in 2026
Emerged as the highest-signal enterprise AI organizational model at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2026 (June 29–July 2, San Francisco). Also the model behind Microsoft Frontier Company at the vendor scale.

## Contrast With Central AI Teams
| | Central AI Team | Forward-Deployed |
|--|--|--|
| Accountability | Features delivered | Business outcomes |
| Feedback loop | Long | Short |
| Adoption risk | High | Low |
