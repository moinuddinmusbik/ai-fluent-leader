# Steinberger Threshold

A term introduced by Nate B. Jones describing the dividing line in the AI-era workforce: **people who direct AI agents** versus **people who get directed by them**. This threshold, not job title or seniority, is the new talent segmentation line for AI-fluent organizations.

## Why It Matters

As AI agents become capable of producing high-volume output, the critical human skill shifts from production to *direction and evaluation*. A person above the Steinberger Threshold can assign an AI agent a correctness-first objective, evaluate the output against that standard, and iterate. A person below it defers to whatever the agent produces and passes it downstream without meaningful judgment.

## Diagnostic Method

Jones recommends a scout mission to reveal where people actually sit: assign a task requiring team members to direct AI agents toward *correct* output — not just high-volume output. Observe who maintains agency over the result and who defers. Title and tenure are poor predictors; the threshold reveals actual [[ai-fluency]].

## Connection to Strike Teams

The Steinberger Threshold is a prerequisite for staffing [[five-person-strike-team|strike teams]] effectively. A strike team operating below the threshold will optimize for volume and ship incorrectness at speed — the worst possible combination.
