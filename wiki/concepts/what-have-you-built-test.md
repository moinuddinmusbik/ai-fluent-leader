# What Have You Built? Test

The emerging universal bar for AI roles: instead of asking about certifications, tools known, or years of experience, interviewers (and hiring managers) ask for a concrete demonstration of something the candidate has actually shipped. Named for the pivotal interview question that Ailin's eventual employer asked her — a question she could answer because she had been building in public.

## Why Traditional Credentials Fail Here

Degrees, bootcamps, and years of job-title experience don't prove the ability to build real AI automations. The "What have you built?" test is direct, unforgeable, and discriminating: either you have a link to point to, or you don't.

## The Implication for Hiring

Organizations that screen for "AI experience" via resume keywords are selecting for credential inflation. Organizations that screen by asking "What have you built? Show me." are selecting for doers.
