# AI Regulatory Headroom

A vendor-selection criterion measuring an AI provider's ability to obtain and maintain regulatory approval to distribute and deploy their models — across geographies and use cases — as governments increase oversight of frontier AI.

## Why It's Become Strategic

For two years the AI race was about who had the most compute and the best model. That story is ending. When a government can decide whether and when a flagship model is allowed to ship, raw technical capability stops being the only thing that wins. A company's investment in regulatory relationships — securing approval to operate — functions like paying for data centers: it's a necessary cost of getting the model out the door.

## Implications for Enterprise Leaders

- Add regulatory headroom to your vendor scorecard alongside benchmark scores.
- Prefer providers with demonstrated government relationships in your operating geographies.
- Assess model distribution restrictions before committing to a provider for long-term workloads.
