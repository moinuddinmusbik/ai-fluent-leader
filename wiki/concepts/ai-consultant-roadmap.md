# AI Consultant Roadmap

Nate Herk's framework for the phase that follows learning a tool like Claude: once you can build, the differentiator stops being the tool and becomes knowing what to build, why, and whether it worked. He calls this next role the AI consultant.

## Doctor vs. Pharmacist

Builders are pharmacists — they hand you exactly what you ask for. Consultants are doctors — they figure out what's actually wrong and then how to fix it. Clients rarely know what they need, only what hurts, which is why the diagnostic role (the doctor) commands the real money.

## Two Roads In

1. **Independent AI consultant** — diagnose and build solutions for outside businesses, positioned as a long-term partner rather than a scrappy automation shop. Trades stability for full ownership of your time and variety of work.
2. **In-house AI consultant** — become the go-to AI person inside one company (e.g. Chief AI Officer or Director of AI). Trades variety for stability and a steady paycheck.

## The 4-Step Roadmap

1. **Audit your own role through a constraint lens** — identify what's actually slowing the team or gating revenue, not just what's repetitive, and write down the specific KPI fixing it would move.
2. **Take on small projects** — scope and build a fix for one item, implement it in a single corner of the business, and turn the result into a case study.
3. **Become an expert at solving problems, not just building** — notice the patterns that repeat across projects.
4. **Formalize the role** — bring leadership the evidence and propose the role yourself; most people won't find this job posted on LinkedIn.

## Key Principle

Constraint first, KPI second, build third. Every project needs to target an actual business constraint and a clear number you're trying to move before you start building — automating something that isn't a real constraint just saves time nobody was waiting on.
