# Creator Trust Stack

A framework introduced by [[nate-b-jones]] (2026-06-20) for what keeps an audience's trust intact once AI tools are part of a creator's or company's production chain. Built around explicit disclosure plus a named, identifiable human who stays accountable for the final shipped result — not a ban on AI tools themselves.

## Why it exists

As AI-assisted content (voice clones, synthetic media) becomes good enough to pass casual scrutiny, trust can no longer rest on an audience's ability to detect "real vs. fake." Nate's stack relocates the trust signal to disclosure practice and ownership rather than detection.

## Connections

- Built on top of [[made-with-ai-five-questions]] — the diagnostic that feeds this operational model.
- Explicitly replaces a blanket "never use AI" policy, which Nate argues doesn't survive contact with how creators actually work.
