# Fable-Style Problem

A term coined by [[nate-b-jones]] for tasks that require broad, strong, generalized intelligence — the opposite of center-of-distribution work (see [[center-edge-distribution]]).

## Characteristics

- The shape of the work is **not obvious** before you begin
- Requires **discovery**: finding the angle in a messy bundle of sources
- Judgment and taste are **load-bearing** (not just execution)
- Often involves **novel domains**: video, physics, character, legal exposure, business strategy in a new market
- You're "asking the model to help you discover the shape of a new type of problem"

## Routing Implication

Fable-style problems belong on Tier 3 (Frontier Model) in the [[job-first-routing]] framework. "There is no substitute for a frontier model right now" when tackling a tough generalized task.

Named after **Fable 5** (Anthropic), which Nate uses as the archetype of a model that enables broad generalized reasoning across multiple domains simultaneously.

## Contrast

- [[center-edge-distribution]]: center = familiar, inspectable, benchmarkable; edge = Fable-style
- **Center:** Write a meeting summary → GLM 5.2 wins  
- **Fable-style:** Understand what a new AI capability means across video/physics/character/legal/business → Frontier only
