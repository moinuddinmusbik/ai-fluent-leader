# AI Hype Cycle

The recurring pattern in AI development: a surge of capability triggers investment and over-promising → hardware/data/research limits cause a crash (AI winter) → quiet research continues on the "written-off" approach → a breakthrough eventually vindicates it → a new surge begins.

## Historical Instances

- **First winter (mid-1970s):** Early symbolic AI over-promised on reasoning tasks. Hardware and data limits caused funding collapse.
- **Second winter (late 1980s):** Expert systems boom collapsed. Neural networks were widely declared dead ends.
- **Deep learning comeback (2012+):** ImageNet 2012 showed neural nets had crossed a capability threshold. The "dead end" approach became the foundation of modern AI.
- **Current wave:** LLMs, agents, and multimodal models represent the post-deep-learning acceleration — but the cycle pattern warns against assuming linear progress.

## Key Events

- **Turing / Enigma (1940s):** Proof of concept that computation can simulate reasoning
- **ImageNet 2012:** Geoffrey Hinton's deep CNN wins by a margin that signals a threshold has been crossed
- **AlphaGo Move 37 (2016):** AI surpasses human intuition in a bounded domain; Lee Sedol retires from professional Go in 2019

## Leadership Relevance

- Leaders who know the pattern can distinguish genuine breakthroughs from hype cycles
- Enables holding nerve during organizational troughs instead of cutting AI budgets prematurely
- Informs contrarian bets on approaches that look like dead ends today but have structural tailwinds
