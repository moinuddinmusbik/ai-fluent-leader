# Five-Person Strike Team

The **five-person strike team** is Nate B. Jones's term for the structural unit of the AI era — a tightly coordinated team of 4–5 people organized around a defined mission, sized to minimize coordination overhead while maximizing the output amplification AI provides.

## The Combinatorics Argument

The formula n(n−1)/2 calculates communication channels in a team. A 5-person team has 10; a 6-person team has 15. AI raised per-person output by roughly an order of magnitude (to ~$2M/year equivalent) while leaving every communication channel at full cost. This means the coordination tax on the sixth team member now costs millions in compounded lost productivity.

## Key Principles

- **Correct over fast:** Strike teams are correctness-first. The bottleneck in the AI era is not how much gets produced but whether it is correct. Strike teams have tight ownership of quality.
- **Defined mission:** Strike teams execute against a known objective with clear parameters. If the mission is unclear or exploratory, this is a [[scout-vs-strike-team|scout]] structure, not a strike team.
- **Ambition expansion:** The structural insight is not doing the same work with fewer people. It is doing dramatically more ambitious work with the same 4–5 people — redirecting coordination savings into mission scope.
