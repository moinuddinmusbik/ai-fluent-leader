# Judgment Visibility

The practice of making **reasoning explicit, documented, and shareable** — so that the quality of your judgment is legible to the people around you, not just implied by outputs.

## Why It Matters

In AI-abundant environments, output quality is no longer a reliable proxy for judgment quality. Judgment visibility is the habit of leaving a trail that shows *why* you made the decisions you made — what you considered, what you rejected, and what tradeoffs you accepted.

## Tactics

- **Build a “judgment layer”**: After using AI output, document your edits, additions, and pushback in a brief note immediately.
- **Apply portable judgment questions**: Before project handoffs or recommendations, explicitly document the reasoning behind key decisions.
- **Whiteboard deliberately**: Use live problem-solving sessions (not just polished presentations) as career development activities.
- **Comprehension documentation**: When reviewing AI-generated work, write a brief “what I would change and why” note even when the output is acceptable.
