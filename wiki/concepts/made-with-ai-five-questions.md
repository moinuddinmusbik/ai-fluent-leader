# Made With AI Is Five Questions

A framework introduced by [[nate-b-jones]] (2026-06-20) that replaces the single binary "is this AI or not?" judgment with a five-part breakdown of what was actually used in producing a piece of content, where in the pipeline it was used, and who is accountable for the final result.

## Why it exists

Voice cloning and other synthetic-media tools are now good enough to pass a half-watching audience, and conversely real human work can fail a casual "is this real?" check. A single yes/no test can't capture either case correctly, so Nate argues the binary detection question should be retired in favor of a structured breakdown.

## Connections

- Companion framework: [[creator-trust-stack]] — the operational model built on top of these five questions.
- Precursor argument referenced in the same episode: there is no blanket "never use AI" rule — disclosure and accountability replace a flat prohibition.
