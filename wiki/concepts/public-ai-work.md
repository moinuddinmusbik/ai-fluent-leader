# Public AI Work

The practice of **running AI-assisted work visibly** — in shared channels, open forums, or declared spaces — so that the reasoning process, not just the output, is legible to the rest of the organization.

## The Problem It Solves

When senior people use AI privately, they generate better outputs but create an apprenticeship gap: junior staff see polished results but never see the judgment calls, prompt strategies, pushback, and reasoning that produced them. This gap widens silently and compounds over years.

Polanyi’s paradox applies: we know more than we can tell. AI outputs share none of the “how.” The only mechanism for transmitting tacit knowledge at scale is visible process.

## The Shopify Model

Shopify’s River agent is designed to run only in public Slack channels. This is not a policy — it is a product architecture choice that makes organizational learning the default, not the exception.

## Four Parts of AI Work Worth Making Visible

1. **Problem framing** — how the question was structured before prompting
2. **Prompt strategy** — what was asked, how, and why
3. **Output evaluation** — what was accepted, rejected, and modified
4. **The use/no-use decision** — when AI output was set aside and human judgment used instead

## Implementation

- Designate a public AI channel; senior people must lead
- Share process, not just prompts — prompt libraries share the “what,” visible process shares the “why”
- For regulated teams: declared spaces with privacy labels enable visibility while managing compliance
