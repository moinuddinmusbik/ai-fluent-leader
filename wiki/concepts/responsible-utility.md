# Responsible Utility

**Introduced by:** [[nate-b-jones]], 2026-07-01
**Source:** [[2026-07-01-nate-b-jones-daily]]

## Definition

Nate B. Jones's term for the design goal of personal AI agents: an agent that is *useful enough to fight the fight* and *careful enough to know whether you told it to*. Responsible utility is the point where capability meets intent discipline — the agent can win, and it knows when it has permission to try.

## Context

Introduced in the "Build Your Own AI Memory" episode in contrast to the Nikita / Lemonade Insurance story (Jan 2026), where an OpenClaw agent won an insurance dispute but without the owner's explicit authorization. The agent crossed the send boundary by guessing intent.

Nate's claim: agents have crossed the capability threshold. They are good enough to win. What they still get wrong is intent — knowing when they are drafting a fight versus starting one. As agents touch more tools (send email, change code, update records), intent becomes one of the central problems of the AI age.

## How To Achieve It

The [[5-part-agent-loop]] (memory, method, boundary, receipt, judgment) is the structural answer. All five components must be present:
- Without **boundary**, the agent guesses when to act.
- Without **receipt**, you can't audit what happened.
- Without **judgment** (the stop-and-ask signal), the agent can't distinguish draft from execute.
