# AI Leadership Pipeline Risk

**Source:** [[wef]] Annual Meeting of the New Champions (Summer Davos), June 23–25, 2026; Harvard workforce research

## Definition

The AI leadership pipeline risk is the structural threat to leadership succession created by AI-driven elimination of entry-level roles. Organizations deploying AI are inadvertently dismantling the apprenticeship layer that has historically produced the next generation of executives.

## The Data (Harvard / WEF, June 2026)

- Entry-level job postings dropped **80% per quarter** since 2023 at organizations adopting generative AI
- Share of entry-level roles in total postings fell from **44% → 38.6%**
- Workers demonstrating AI competency earn a **56% wage premium** over peers
- Global workforce requires reskilling within **four years**

## Why This Is a Strategic Risk

Traditional leadership development:
1. Junior → Associate → Manager → Senior → Director → VP → C-Suite

The entry-level layer is where people learn to:
- Break down complex problems
- Manage workload and deadlines
- Learn from senior colleagues (apprenticeship)
- Build judgment through consequence

AI is eliminating precisely these roles. Organizations are therefore:
- Reducing the talent pipeline volume entering the leadership funnel
- Removing the cognitive apprenticeship that builds future leaders
- Creating a bifurcated talent market with no clear pathway for non-AI-fluent workers

## Strategic Response

1. **Design hybrid "AI copilot" roles** that rebuild the apprenticeship layer with AI integration from the start — replace eliminated entry-level roles with AI-augmented entry-level roles, not vacuums
2. **Accelerated pathways** for mid-career professionals with AI fluency — the wage premium signal confirms the market has priced this
3. **Board-level succession planning** that accounts for pipeline reduction as an AI risk item, not just an HR metric
