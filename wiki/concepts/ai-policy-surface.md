# AI Policy Surface

A concept introduced by [[nate-b-jones]] on 2026-06-13 to describe the emerging reality that **frontier model access has become a policy lever** — a variable governments and regulators can flip, on any timeline, independently of model quality, commercial relationships, or prior access guarantees.

## Core Idea

Before June 2026, frontier AI model access was essentially a commercial variable: you could use the best available model if you had the money and met the provider’s terms. Quality was the gating factor.

The Fable 5 shutdown (June 12, 2026) established a new reality: governments can now treat frontier model access as a national security policy surface — activating export controls, foreign national restrictions, or supply chain risk designations that make entire model families unavailable globally, with zero notice and no migration window.

## Mechanism

The US government’s export control directive against Fable 5 and Mythos 5 demonstrated how this surface works:
1. A national security concern is raised (e.g., a jailbreak claim)
2. A government directive applies broad export control language (e.g., “foreign nationals”)
3. The language is too broad for surgical compliance, forcing global shutdown
4. The “off switch” is pulled for all users worldwide — not just the restricted class

## Implications for Leaders

- AI model availability is now a multi-dimensional variable: capability + cost + **policy risk**
- Single-model dependency is a supply chain continuity risk, not merely a vendor preference
- AI vendor risk assessment must include: export control exposure, government classification status, litigation history, and geopolitical relationship
