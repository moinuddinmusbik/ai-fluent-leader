# Voice AI as Next Interface

The emerging shift from text-based AI interactions to natural-language voice interfaces as the primary layer for client-facing AI systems. Flagged by Nate Herk as a dedicated session topic at AIS Live (July 2026): "Where natural-language AI is heading and what your clients are about to expect."

## Key Aspects

- **Current state:** AI automation practitioners are building primarily text-pipeline and workflow systems (n8n, Claude Code, make.com). Voice AI is the next billable layer clients will expect.
- **Client-facing use case:** The AIS Live Day 1 agenda features a live build of "a voice AI that books clients" — suggesting the first production use case is outbound/inbound sales and appointment-setting.
- **Interface trajectory:** The session title is "Voice as the next interface" — not voice as a feature, but as the primary way end-users interact with AI-powered systems. The implication: builders who don't have voice in their stack will be behind the next wave of client expectations.
- **Adjacent signal:** Jannis Moore (Workless speaker) "builds and teaches voice AI systems" — corroborating that voice is now a practitioner specialty, not an experimental category.
