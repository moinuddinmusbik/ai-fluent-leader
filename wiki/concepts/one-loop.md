# The One Loop

Nate B. Jones's five-part minimum unit for **responsible agent utility** — the smallest structure that allows an agent to act on your behalf without guessing at your intent.

Introduced: [[2026-07-14-nate-b-jones-daily]]  
Related: [[owned-stack]] · [[agent-intent]] · [[nate-b-jones]]

## The Five Parts

1. **Memory** — Persistent context the agent retains about you, your standards, and your recurring situations
2. **Method** — The defined procedure (skill) the agent executes; not improvised each session
3. **Boundary** — The explicit line between drafting an action and taking it; must be defined before the agent touches external systems
4. **Receipt** — A verifiable log of what the agent did and why it believed it had permission
5. **Judgment** — The agent's rule for stopping and asking when the boundary is ambiguous

## Why It Matters

Without all five parts, an agent that is highly capable will still act on the wrong version of your intent — and it will do it fast. The One Loop is the answer to the [[agent-intent]] problem: it keeps a capable agent pointed at what you actually meant.
