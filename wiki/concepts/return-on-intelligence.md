# Return on Intelligence

## Definition

Return on Intelligence (ROI-as-Intelligence) is a C-suite measurement framework that tracks how AI augments leadership decision-making — measuring decision speed, decision quality, and decision scope — rather than reducing AI's value to cost savings or headcount reduction.

Introduced as the successor to traditional ROI in the context of AI in [[weekly-ai-leadership-stories]] (July 13–19, 2026 digest), sourced from a CEOWORLD Magazine analysis (July 13, 2026).

## Key Aspects

- **Decision leverage:** How many better decisions per quarter does AI enable?
- **Decision quality:** What is the measurable improvement in outcomes when AI augments a specific decision type?
- **Decision scope:** How many decision types (previously unavailable or too slow) has AI unlocked?
- **Accountability structure:** Organizations with clearly defined accountability for AI ROI are significantly more likely to demonstrate established ROI — the differentiator is ownership, not technology spend.

## Why It Matters

Only 7% of leaders can demonstrate established AI ROI (KPMG Q2 2026). CFO reviews of AI spend are intensifying in H2 2026. Leaders who still measure AI purely in cost/headcount terms will lose the CFO conversation in Q3 2026. The shift from ROI-as-cost to Return on Intelligence requires building new measurement dashboards tied to business outcomes rather than deployment counts.
