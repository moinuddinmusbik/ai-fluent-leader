# Dark Factory Approach

A framework for filtering AI-assisted knowledge work by the **outcome** required, not the **output** produced. Named by analogy to dark factories in manufacturing — where automation runs without human presence, optimized for results rather than activity.

## The Core Idea

The benchmark of value for AI-assisted knowledge work is not how much output was generated (documents written, emails drafted, analyses run) but whether that output changes a decision or enables a specific action. If it doesn't, the AI is automating activity — not results.

## The Outcome Filter

Before approving or expanding AI-assisted workflows, apply the dark factory test:

1. **State the outcome** — what decision, action, or result will this output enable?
2. **Quantify the change** — can you measure the outcome (latency reduced, decisions accelerated, errors caught)?
3. **Reject output-only justifications** — "the AI will generate more reports" is not an outcome; "the AI will cut the time to investment committee from 5 days to 2" is.

## Connection to Agentic Pipelines

The dark factory approach pairs with the [[agent-harness]] concept: once you know the outcome you need, you can architect an agentic pipeline (not just isolated agents) designed to drive that outcome end-to-end, with measurable checkpoints.
