# Model Replacement Test

An evaluation exercise — proposed by Nate B. Jones — that answers: *Can the work I have already tuned move to a cheaper model without starting over?*

Run before any major AI contract renewal when cheaper alternatives (e.g., K3-class hosted services) enter the market.

## The Exercise

1. Identify your highest-value AI workloads — the tasks where you've tuned prompts, built pipelines, or fine-tuned a model.
2. Run those exact workloads against a candidate cheaper model.
3. Measure output quality, latency, and cost.
4. Determine rebuild cost if quality is insufficient (prompt re-engineering, fine-tuning, integration changes).
5. Compare total migration cost vs. ongoing cost premium of staying on the current provider.

## Why It Matters

Cheaper models benefit your competitors equally. The test reveals whether you have a portable, provider-agnostic capability (a moat) or a sunk cost that locks you to one provider's pricing. Leaders who run this test proactively use new entrants as pricing leverage; those who don't discover the answer when it's too late to act.
