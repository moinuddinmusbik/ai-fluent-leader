# Konecta

Global customer-experience (CX) and digital-services provider; 25 years of CX expertise, more than one million daily customer resolutions across 500 clients.

## Key Facts

- Operates across complex, regulated sectors: banking, telecom, energy, mobility, retail, and travel
- ISO 42001-certified AI management framework underpins its agentic AI platform

## Kolibri Launch (June 16, 2026)

- Launched Kolibri, an agentic AI orchestration platform aimed at pulling enterprises out of "pilot purgatory."
- Ships with a library of cross-industry and sector-specific use cases that are up to 80% pre-built and pre-tested, with the remaining 20% tailored to each client's systems — designed to compress deployment from months to weeks.
- Targets regulated sectors where compliance burden usually stalls agentic AI pilots from reaching production.
- Built on an ISO 42001-certified AI management framework with cybersecurity controls, compliance policies, observability, and real-time audit trails built into every deployment, not bolted on afterward.
- Ready-to-deploy use cases span billing, technical support, appointment booking, claims handling, and order tracking — agents that act (update records, process transactions) rather than just respond, with every decision logged and auditable.

## Relevance to AI-Fluent Leadership

Kolibri is a concrete instance of the "factory model" pattern already tracked under [[enterprise-ai-deployment]] (NTT DATA / Google Cloud, June 2026): pre-built, reusable agent assets reduce time-to-production in regulated industries, parallel to NTT DATA's reusable agent assets for industry verticals. Konecta's audit-trail-by-design approach is also a direct example of the "control built in, not bolted on" principle IBM is pushing under [[enterprise-ai-governance]] the same week.
