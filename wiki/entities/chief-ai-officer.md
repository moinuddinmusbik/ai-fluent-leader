# Chief AI Officer (CAIO)

## Description

The Chief AI Officer (CAIO) is a C-suite role responsible for owning an organization's AI strategy, governance, and transformation. Originally a "figureheads" role promoting AI adoption, the CAIO has evolved by 2026 into an operator of enterprise transformation — driving AI from pilot to production at scale.

## Key Facts

- **Adoption surge:** 76% of organizations have a CAIO in 2026, up from 26% in 2025 (IBM IBV CEO Study, 2,000 CEOs, 33 geographies, Feb–Apr 2026)
- **ROI impact:** Organizations with a CAIO achieve 5% higher return on AI investments; AI projects under CAIO leadership are nearly twice as likely to remain in production for 3+ years
- **Production success:** CAIO oversight raises the rate of GenAI prototypes moving to full production from 36% to 44%
- **Influence trajectory:** All surveyed CEOs at organizations with a CAIO expect the role's influence to increase by 2030
- **Scope:** Present not just in tech companies (Meta, Salesforce) but in enterprises like Heineken, WPP, Nike, CVS Health

## Role Evolution (2025 → 2026)

| Then (2025) | Now (2026) |
|---|---|
| AI evangelist / figurehead | Transformation operator |
| Promoting AI adoption | Moving pilots to production at scale |
| Technology-focused | P&L and outcome-focused |
| Separate from C-suite | Operating partner to CEO, CFO, Chief of Staff |

## Key Responsibilities in 2026

- Own the AI [[return-on-intelligence]] dashboard
- Design and enforce [[tiered-agent-governance]] frameworks
- Bridge between AI initiatives and board-level decision-making (in partnership with the Chief of Staff)
- Manage the AI budget and demonstrate ROI to CFO
- Drive organizational readiness — 83% of CEOs say AI success depends more on people's adoption than technology (IBM)
