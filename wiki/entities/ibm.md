# IBM

Enterprise technology company; publisher of AI governance and sovereignty research through the IBM Institute for Business Value (IBV), and vendor of the watsonx.governance platform.

## Key Facts

- IBM Institute for Business Value (IBV) regularly surveys senior executives on enterprise AI maturity, dependency, and governance
- watsonx.governance is IBM's AI governance product line, being repositioned from periodic review toward continuous "AI assurance"

## AI Sovereignty Study (June 16, 2026)

- "The Calculus of AI Sovereignty" study (1,000 senior executives globally) found just 9% say they fully understand their organization's AI dependencies across vendors, models, and infrastructure.
- 71% say switching their primary AI vendor or model would be difficult; 68% say meeting data residency and sovereignty requirements across jurisdictions is challenging.
- Organizations with stronger control over the AI stack protect 55% more operating profit from disruption than peers, yet only 7% of surveyed organizations operate at that level of maturity.
- 73% describe their AI environments as "intentionally multi-vendor," but IBM's research suggests this diversity is often accidental — driven by organizational silos rather than deliberate resilience strategy.
- IBM frames AI sovereignty as "not owning the stack — it's staying in control when conditions change," positioning flexibility and switchability, not self-hosting, as the real strategic asset.

## Governance to Assurance — Think 2026 (June 16, 2026)

- Per Grant Thornton's 2026 AI Impact Survey, cited by IBM at Think 2026, 78% of business executives are unsure they could pass an independent AI governance audit within 90 days.
- 46% of leaders attribute their AI underperformance directly to weak controls and compliance gaps, not model capability.
- IBM IBV estimates a $20B enterprise loses roughly $140 million a year to AI irregularities.
- IBM previewed the next generation of watsonx.governance, reframing governance from a "periodic review process" into continuous "AI assurance" built on visibility (including shadow AI), control (guardrails and deployment policy), and accountability (live metrics tied to controls and owners).
- One in two operations leaders say they need a formalized AI strategy or governance framework within the next six months.

## Relevance to AI-Fluent Leadership

IBM's two June 2026 reports quantify the gap this wiki tracks under [[enterprise-ai-governance]] and [[enterprise-ai-deployment]]: enterprises are scaling AI deployment faster than they are building the visibility and control to govern it. The $140M/year loss figure and the 90-day audit-readiness gap give AI-fluent leaders board-ready, P&L-denominated language for funding governance work before it becomes a compliance failure.
